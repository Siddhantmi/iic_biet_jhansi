Setu Nirman

    June 20, 2021 Venue - IIC Hall

An idea that is not dangerous is unworthy of being called an idea at all.

What is Setu Nirman?

We have been using bridges for centuries to cross the obstacles such as valleys, rough terrain or bodies of water. To make the bridge last longer, it has to be made more strong and durable. In this event we are going to conduct a competition of constructing a truss bridge using popsicle sticks of standard dimensions and the strength of the bridges will be examined by applying load to the centre of the top side of the bridge.


Purpose

  • Learn about dimensional analysis.
  • Learn about engineering design.
  • Learn about planning and construction.
  • Learn about teamwork and working in groups.
  • Learn about truss bridges and different types of bridges.

    • Useful links: https://youtu.be/2M84QWQ-mHk https://youtu.be/FMn4XmziaUI


    Flow of Event

    • The participants will be given complete details about the event and its rules, score counting procedure, negatives, and elimination for the smooth conduction of the event.
    • Participants will be making team comprising 4 -5 members.
    • Working time can be flexible, depending upon the fcomplexity of the structure designing.
    • Participants will be making team comprising 4 -5 members.
    • After the completion of the working time,Teams will be presenting their models for Testing Round.
    • In this round the submitted structure will be tested on basis of its design and load withstanding capability.
    • The team whose structure withstands the maximum pressure and does not collapse under simulated conditions will be considered winner. The winning team will be awarded certificates, medals and sponsor rewards.

    Rules and Regulations of the event

    • This is team event. Each team should have 4 or 5 members.
    • The winning bridge is that which holds the largest load.
    • The bridges will be weighted (number of popsicle sticks) before testing. In the case of a tie, the lightest bridge will be declared the winner.
    • Laminating is allowed to make the thin sticks thicker. be broken as part of testing.
    • Loads will be applied to the centre of the top side of the bridge.
    • The load will be applied till the bridge is completely broken.
    • The bridges will be loaded from the top, the loading bar will be placed roughly over the center.
    • The use of plastic glue/ hot glue/glue gun is strictly prohibited.
    • Any paint or any other coating aside from glue is not allowed.
    • Use of prohibited items and ill-treatment with coordinators and jury members may pave the way for your disqualification.
    • Write the name of your team members, branch, year on a slip attached with your bridge.

    Structure constraints

    • Bridge length must be in range (min 400 mm – max 500 mm).
    • Bridge width must be in range (min 100mm – max 150 mm).
    • Bridge height must not be greater than 150 mm
    • For an overview visit: https://youtu.be/2M84QWQ-mHk https://youtu.be/FMn4XmziaUI

    Material Allowed

    • Each participant, or team will work with maximum 300 popsicle sticks(standard size 4.5 inch)..
    • White paper.
    • All purpose, nontoxic glue (note: crazy and industrial glue are not permitted). White glue is the only glue permitted (e.g. fevicol MR, fevicol SH, wooden fevicol).
    • Hot glue (glue gun) is strictly prohibited.
